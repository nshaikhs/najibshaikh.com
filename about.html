About

Pakistan to New York, via economics, startups, and a lot of building.

I grew up in the UAE, attending the International School of Choueifat. The kind of school where discipline was the curriculum and you learned early that nobody was going to figure things out for you.

Started in banking. Quickly figured out I wanted to be inside the companies, not advising them from the outside. Moved to New York in 2020, right as everything shut down. Perfect timing to join a fast-growing telecom startup. I've been here since, building companies and figuring out how to make things work in places where nothing exists yet.

Based in the NYC area now. Married to Elena. First baby on the way. That last part still doesn't feel entirely real.

Outside of work.

Die-hard Real Madrid fan. If there's a match on, nothing else exists.

I've been collecting watches since college. Everything from a Casio F91W (still the best beater watch ever made) to a Tissot PRX. The collection tells a story of every phase of life.

I cook Pakistani food, usually badly at first and then better after enough repetitions. Biryani took the longest to get right but was worth every failed attempt.

Played varsity basketball, still pick up games when I can. Got into padel recently. Swimming is the thing I do when I need to think clearly about something hard.

On the car side: I follow the SUV and EV markets more closely than most people follow sports. The intersection of engineering, design, and utility is endlessly interesting to me.
