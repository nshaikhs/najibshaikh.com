# Site Review: najibshaikh.com
**Reviewer**: Senior Creative Director (500+ executive portfolios reviewed)
**Date**: 2026-04-03
**Verdict**: FLAG

---

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong portfolio site. The design is polished. The writing is better than 90% of operator portfolios I see. The blog essays are genuinely good, particularly "The $280M Revenue Growth I Can't Take Credit For," which is the single best piece on the site and the most honest thing I've read from an operator in years.

That said, there are real problems that should be fixed before this goes in front of a hiring manager at an AI company. The main issues: (1) a naming inconsistency between the index.html project card and the actual project page that will confuse visitors, (2) the Retailo work card omits the starting revenue figure that appears everywhere else, creating a math gap the reader has to fill themselves, (3) the project case study page for "Chief of Staff" still uses the old product name in the page title and meta description, contradicting the index.html card that calls it "AI Operating System for SMBs," (4) the nav structure on project pages is inconsistent with the main site and blog pages, and (5) some copy across the site is doing more telling than showing.

## SECTION-BY-SECTION: index.html

### 1. Meta / OG Tags

**Exact text (OG description):**
> "I build and scale everything outside of product. $280M+ in revenue growth driven. Four companies worth $1.5B+ combined."

**Rating: 6/10**

**Issues:**
- "Revenue growth driven" is a strange phrasing. Nobody says this. It reads like a LinkedIn auto-generated summary.
- "Four companies worth $1.5B+ combined" is a claim you can't really substantiate and a reader might push back on. What is each company's valuation? Who valued them? If these are private companies, "worth" is doing heavy lifting. The hero section itself uses "Combined valuations" which is more honest framing.
- The OG title says "Operator & Scaler" but the page title says "Operator & Builder." Pick one.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "I build and scale everything outside of product. $20M to $150M. $80M to $230M. Two-time Chief of Staff. Now building with AI."

**Twitter card description:**
> "I build and scale everything outside of product. $20M to $150M. $80M to $230M. Four companies worth $1.5B+."

Same issue. At least the Twitter card shows the actual revenue ranges, which is better. But "Four companies worth $1.5B+" should be reconsidered.

### 2. Hero Section

**Hero label:**
> "0 to 1 . Operator . 10x with AI"

**Rating: 7/10**

"10x with AI" is the weakest of the three. It's a buzzword. Everyone claims 10x. The other two descriptors ("0 to 1" and "Operator") are specific and differentiated. "10x with AI" is not.

**Suggested rewrite:** "0 to 1 . Operator . AI Practitioner" or "0 to 1 . Operator . 7 AI Products Shipped"

---

**Hero title:**
> "I join early and build what doesn't exist yet."

**Rating: 9/10**

This is excellent. Specific, confident, differentiated. It immediately tells a hiring manager what they're getting. It also functions as a filter: if you need someone to optimize an existing machine, this person isn't it. That's smart positioning.

---

**Hero subtitle:**
> "2x Chief of Staff. 2x Head of Strategy & Ops. $100M+ raised. 3 continents. Now deep in AI. 7 products shipped."

**Rating: 7/10**

The facts are good but the construction is choppy. Six separate claims in quick succession. The reader's brain starts glazing after the third one. "Now deep in AI" is the weakest phrase. It's vague. Deep how? Compared to what?

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Two-time Chief of Staff. Two-time Head of Strategy & Ops. $100M+ raised across 3 continents. Shipped 7 AI products in the past year."

The change: spell out "Two-time" instead of "2x" (more polished for executive audiences), collapse "3 continents" into the raise (it's context, not a standalone claim), and make the AI claim specific by adding the timeframe.

---

**Hero stats:**
- $280M+ Revenue growth
- $100M+ Capital raised
- $1.5B+ Combined valuations
- 4 Companies scaled

**Rating: 7/10**

**Issues:**
- **$280M+ revenue growth**: The blog post "The $280M Revenue Growth I Can't Take Credit For" explicitly argues you shouldn't take credit for this number. Putting it as the first, largest stat on the hero creates a tension the reader will notice if they read the blog. This is a strategic choice: either own the number or don't. Right now the site does both, which undermines the honesty of the essay.
- **$1.5B+ Combined valuations**: Where does this come from? US Mobile, Retailo, Ad-Shield, and the stealth AI company. What are their individual valuations? If these are unverifiable private company numbers, this stat is a liability. A skeptical hiring manager will do the math and wonder.
- **"Revenue growth"** is ambiguous. The blog essays use the much more honest framing "revenue growth during tenure." The hero stat implies causation. The essays correctly argue against it.

**Suggested fix:** Change "Revenue growth" to "Revenue growth during tenure" or "Revenue scaled during tenure." It's the same number but honest framing.

## AI-TELL SENTENCES
